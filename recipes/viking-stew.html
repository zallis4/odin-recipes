Viking Stew

A hearty stew made with winter staples, just like móðir used to make.

  • 1 pound venison, cubed
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 2 parsnips, chopped
  • 2 turnips, chopped
  • 4 cups broth
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 teaspoon thyme, dried
  • 1 teaspoon sage, dried
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary, dried
  1. In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until softened.
  2. Add the meat and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Stir in the root vegetables, salt, pepper, thyme, sage, and rosemary.
  4. Pour in the broth, bring to a boil, and then reduce the heat. Simmer for 1-2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  5. Serve hot with a side of rustic bread.
