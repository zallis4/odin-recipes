Cold Cured Trout

A riverside favorite made with foraged herbs and juniper berries.

  • 1 pound trout filets, skin on
  • 1/4 cup salt
  • 1/4 sugar
  • 1 tablespoon crushed juniper berries
  • 1 large bunch fresh dill
  • 1 tablespoon pepper
  1. In a small bowl, combine the salt, sugar, juniper berries, and pepper.
  2. Spread half of the dill on a large sheet of butcher's paper. Place the trout filets skin-side down on the dill, then cover the filets with the salt mixture.
  3. Top the trout with the remaining dill and tightly bundle in the butcher's paper.
  4. Refrigerate the wrapped trout for 24-48 hours, turning the fillets every 12 hours to ensure even curing.
  5. After uring, remove trout from the butcher's paper and gently scrape off the dill and curing mixture.
  6. Thinly slice and serve with a dollop of mustard sauce or soured cream.
